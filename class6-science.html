üå±Chapter 1: Food ‚Äî Where Does It Come From

Food comes from plants and animals. Plants provide grains, fruits, vegetables and oils; animals provide milk, eggs and meat.

  • Parts of plants used as food: roots (carrot), stems (potato), leaves (spinach), fruits (apple), seeds (rice).
  • Food chains: producers (plants) ‚Üí consumers (herbivores) ‚Üí carnivores ‚Üí decomposers.

üî¨ Chapter 2: Components of Food

Food contains nutrients: carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals, water and fiber. Each has a role.

  • Carbohydrates provide energy (rice, bread).
  • Proteins help growth and repair (dal, eggs).
  • Vitamins & minerals keep body healthy; deficiency causes diseases (vitamin C ‚Üí scurvy).
  • Balanced diet = right amounts of all nutrients.

üßµ Chapter 3: Fibre to Fabric

Fibres come from plants (cotton) and animals (wool). Process: fibre ‚Üí yarn (spinning) ‚Üí fabric (weaving).

  • Cotton: from cotton plant; wool: from sheep. Silk: from silkworm cocoons.
  • Processing includes ginning, spinning, weaving and finishing.

üíß Chapter 4: Water

Water is essential. It cycles through evaporation, condensation and precipitation (water cycle).

  • Sources: rivers, lakes, groundwater.
  • Importance: drinking, agriculture, cleaning and habitat for organisms.
